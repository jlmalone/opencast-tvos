OpenCast

FCast-compatible receiver for Apple TV — Privacy Policy & App Information

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

OpenCast does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data — ever.

What this app does

OpenCast listens on your local network for incoming media streams from FCast-compatible sender apps and plays them through your Apple TV. All communication happens entirely on your local network between your devices.

Data we collect

None. Specifically:

  • No account or sign-in required
  • No analytics or crash reporting
  • No advertising identifiers
  • No location data
  • No usage tracking
  • No data is sent to any server — by us or any third party

Local network access

The app uses your local network exclusively to receive media streams from sender devices on the same network. It advertises itself via Bonjour/mDNS so sender apps can discover it automatically. No data leaves your local network.

Third-party libraries

This app uses TVVLCKit (LGPL 2.1) for broad format support and the WebRTC framework (BSD 3-Clause) for screen mirroring. Neither library collects or transmits user data.

Children's privacy

Because we collect no data from anyone, there are no special considerations for children.

Changes to this policy

If anything changes, this page will be updated. The app collects no data and this is unlikely to change.
